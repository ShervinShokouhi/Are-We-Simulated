# Are We Simulated?
Try to recap Truth, even its just a projection!

This repository presents a conceptual exploration of the **simulation hypothesis**, proposing a two-layer model of the universe based on a set of foundational assumptions.

## Overview

The text develops a structured argument suggesting that the physical universe (matter, energy, time, and distance) can be understood as a **projection** of an underlying **abstract layer** composed of data and processing. In this view, physical laws, causality, and fundamental constants emerge as reflections of computational rules and constraints operating at a deeper level of reality.

## Key Assumptions

The model begins with six core assumptions:

1. The physical world consists of **matter and energy**.
2. Matter and energy can **affect one another**.
3. Matter and energy interact within the **medium of time**.
4. **Distance** influences the interaction between matter and energy.
5. An **abstract world** exists, consisting of data and processes occurring in a lower layer.
6. Matter, energy, and time serve as **projection tools** for the results of data and processing in the outer (physical) layer.

## The Two-Layer Model

| Layer | Description | Nature |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Lower Layer (Abstract Universe)** | Composed of data and processing; governed by laws of information and computation | Fundamental |
| **Outer Layer (Physical Universe)** | Composed of matter, energy, time, and distance; a projection of lower-layer processing results | Emergent / Displayed |

## Implications

If the physical world is a projection of underlying data processes, several conclusions follow:

- The **laws of physics** correspond to processing rules in the data layer.
- Physical constraints (e.g., the **speed of light** as a causality limit) may reflect computational limitations or data-update mechanisms.
- **Time and distance** are not intrinsic but serve as methods to represent data dependencies (e.g., locality or causal order).
- **Matter and energy** emerge from stable data patterns, akin to objects in a computer simulation appearing on a physical display.

## Alignment with Modern Physics

The framework resonates with several established and speculative theories:

- **Holographic Principle** — Information within a volume of space can be encoded on its boundary.
- **Digital Physics** — The universe may be the output of a vast computational process.
- **Quantum Field Theory** — Matter and interactions are described through states of an underlying informational field.

## Central Question

> *Is it possible to alter the physical world by manipulating the data layer?*

This open question invites further theoretical and philosophical investigation.

## Summary

> The physical world is a dynamic, law-governed display rooted in a more fundamental computation.
